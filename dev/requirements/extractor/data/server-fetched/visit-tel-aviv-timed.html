Findings from the Present

Findings from the Present – A Rolling Conversation in the “Heritage of the Future” Exhibition

A rolling conversation around the four central themes of the “Heritage of the Future” exhibition, joined by the exhibiting architects and curators, Friedrich von Borries and Sabrina Cegla. Rather than asking how the future will look, the exhibition asks how our present will be seen from there – a fresh perspective on everyday architecture and the culture we’re creating here and now, often without realizing it.

Architecture and the built environment carry the stories and beliefs of the time they were created. They represent culture, political conflicts, power structures, and technological, economic, and ecological conditions. In the exhibition “The Heritage of the Future,” we ask not what the future will look like, but how our present will be read from within it and what we leave for generations to come. What role does architecture play not only as a physical force shaping the built environment, but also as a cultural, political, and social mechanism that influences how we imagine the future, interpret the past, and experience the present?


For the exhibition opening, we will hold rolling conversations on its four central themes, every hour on the hour. This is an invitation to reflect on the present from a future perspective – to imagine. Not to escape reality, but to challenge what is taken for granted. When the present is viewed as a future archaeological layer, even the everyday – the square, the sign, the station, the street, and concepts like technology and progress – become charged with new meaning. What of all this will be preserved, what will disappear, and what will one day tell our story?

The conversation will be held in English, in the presence of the exhibiting architects and the exhibition’s curators, Friedrich von Borries and Sabrina Cegla.
