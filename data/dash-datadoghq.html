Adam Scott stars in the Emmy-nominated Apple TV+ series, “Severance,” for which he also serves as executive producer. For his performance, he's received an Emmy nomination for “Outstanding Actor in a Drama Series", Golden Globe Award nominations for “Best Performance by an Actor in a Television Series – Drama”, Critics' Choice Award nominations for “Best Actor in a Drama Series”, as well as nominations for a Screen Actors Guild Award, and Film Independent Spirit Award. As a companion to the series, Scott co-hosts “The Severance Podcast with Ben Stiller and Adam Scott,” that he produces through his Great Scott Productions. He most recently wrapped production on “The Whisper Man” opposite Robert De Niro, Michelle Monaghan and Michael Keaton. Up next, Scott will appear in NEON's supernatural horror film “Hokum”, which will be released on May 1st, 2026. Additionally, he stars opposite Danielle Deadwyler and Greg Kinnear in “The Saviors”, which premiered at SXSW 2026. The film is directed by Kevin Hamedani and produced through Great Scott Productions.

Adam Scott

Prior to founding Datadog, Olivier Pomel built data systems for K-12 teachers as a VP, Technology for Wireless Generation, where he grew the development team from a handful of people to close to 100 of the best engineers in New York until the company’s acquisition by News Corp. Before Wireless Generation, Olivier held software engineering positions at IBM Research and several internet startups. Olivier is an original author of the VLC media player and holds a MS, CS from the Ecole Centrale Paris.

Olivier Pomel

CEO & Co-Founder Datadog

Prior to founding Datadog, Alexis Lê-Quôc served as the Director of Operations for Wireless Generation, where he built the team and infrastructure that served more than four million students in 49 states. As a member of the original “devops” movement, Alexis spent several years as a software engineer at IBM Research, Neomeo, and Orange, and he brings a strong focus on technical elegance and operational efficiency to Datadog. Alexis holds an MS, CS from the Ecole Centrale Paris and has presented sessions on cloud monitoring and server performance at many conferences, including AWS re:Invent, Monitorama, DevOpsDays, Velocity, and PyCon.

Alexis Lê-Quôc

Prior to Google, Li held multiple executive leadership positions at VMware, including Senior Vice President and General Manager for the Storage and Availability Business Unit. Li holds a Ph.D degree from Princeton University, a master's degree from Cornell University and a bachelor’s degree from Tsinghua University. She completed the Stanford Executive Program from Stanford Graduate School of Business in 2014.
